Medical Imaging and Diagnostic Algorithms

Artificial Intelligence Working Group

A multidisciplinary research and development unit aiming to develop AI-supported diagnostic and decision support algorithms for the analysis of medical imaging data

About Our Working Group

Medical imaging can be considered as the eyes of modern medicine. Just like a detective examining clues at a crime scene, radiologists and other specialists search for traces of diseases through images.

This process is both a highly precise and time-consuming field of expertise. This is where artificial intelligence comes into play, serving as a powerful assistant that can detect details invisible to the human eye and analyze millions of images in seconds.

Our group's primary goal is to make imaging-based diagnostic processes faster, more precise, and more accessible. This process is not just a technological advancement but also means the democratization of healthcare services.

Research and Development Focus Areas

Key research topics we focus on for the development of AI technologies in medical imaging

Image Segmentation

We develop algorithms that mimic the human brain's ability to distinguish anatomical structures. We create sophisticated systems that answer the question "which organ does this pixel belong to?" for every pixel.

Challenge: Since each patient's anatomy is slightly different, the system needs to continuously adapt.

Multimodal Image Integration

We combine information from different imaging methods like CT, MR, PET to reveal the complete picture of the disease. This process is like different musical instruments coming together in a symphony.

Innovation: Precise alignment of images taken at different times and positions

Radiomics Analysis

We extract quantitative features from images that the human eye cannot discern. Characteristics like tumor heterogeneity, symmetry, and surface roughness are characterized with mathematical parameters.

Value: Clues about tumor aggressiveness and likelihood of treatment response

Explainable AI

To enable doctors to trust the algorithm's decisions, we present the rationale for these decisions in an understandable way. It's critical that the system can explain not just the correct answer but how it reached that conclusion.

Criticality: In medicine, misdiagnosis can be a matter of life and death

Human-Machine Collaboration

We research hybrid working models where AI will support human experts. Like modern aircraft piloting: automatic systems take control, the pilot makes critical decisions.

Future: Radiologists will evaluate AI preliminary analyses and make final decisions

Real-Time Analysis

We develop systems capable of instant analysis while images are being taken for surgical applications and emergencies. Like a live concert's sound technician: ability to make adjustments instantly while music is playing.

Application: Immediate detection of situations requiring emergency intervention
